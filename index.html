Short Bio

I received my Ph.D. degree in mechanical engineering from Concordia University, Montréal, Québec, Canada, in 2016. I was a Horizon Postdoctoral Fellow with Concordia University for two years. Since 2019, I have been an Associate Professor with the College of Automation Engineering, Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics, China.

My research interests are in all aspects of condition monitoring, fault detection and diagnosis (FDD), fault-tolerant control (FTC) and fault-tolerant cooperative control (FTCC) when applied to problems in energy systems. Specific research areas include: wind turbines and wind farms, hybrid power systems, large-scale renewable power integration to the grid, smart grids, and smart city.
