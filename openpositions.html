Doctoral Researcher:

We have a Doctoral Researcher position in “Advanced Control for Resilient Cyber-Physical Renewable Energy Systems.” This represents an exciting opportunity to engage in innovative research at the intersection of advanced control systems, cyber-physical systems, and renewable energies (wind, solar, microgrids, etc.)

Job description:

  • As renewable energy systems are becoming increasingly digital, smarter, interconnected and thus more efficient, they are facing new and complex challenges related to their physical and cyber components. Due to these rapid evolutions and the new threats of cyber-attacks, these systems’ physical and cyber resilience must be improved. In response to these challenges, the successful candidate for this Doctoral Researcher position will engage in cutting-edge research to develop novel control schemes that are uniquely designed to be both fault-tolerant and attack-resilient. The focus will be on integrating advanced reinforcement learning and adaptive control techniques to enable real-time control adaptation and system reconfiguration. The goal is to proactively address and mitigate the impacts of faults and cyber-attacks before they escalate into major disruptions. This research is crucial for enhancing the resilience and reliability of renewable energy systems, fostering greater trust and wider adoption. Such advancements are key to increasing investments in these technologies, essential in the global effort to combat climate change and secure a sustainable energy future. The position also involves collaboration within a multidisciplinary team to push the boundaries of current knowledge and technology in this field, publishing research findings in reputable journals, presenting at international conferences, and possibly including a few months of mobility to a Canadian partner during the research stages for collaborative work.

Requirements:

Applicants are expected to meet the following criteria:

  • Educational Qualification: Must hold a Master’s degree in systems and control, electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, or a related field at the time of application.

  • Academic Background: A solid background in control systems is necessary. Additional knowledge in optimization, reinforcement learning, cybersecurity, and renewable energy systems is highly desirable.

  • Technical Proficiency: Strong skills in MATLAB or Python-based programming are essential. Candidates should also be willing to further develop their MATLAB/Simulink skills.

  • Language Skills: Proficiency in both written and spoken English is required. Applicants should possess excellent communication and interpersonal skills. Proficiency in Finnish is not mandatory.

  • Working Style: Ability to work both independently and as part of a team. Candidates should demonstrate a balance of autonomy and collaborative skills.

  • Additional Competencies: A strong willingness to engage deeply in the research field of cyber-physical systems is highly appreciated. An interest in pursuing a research career and the capacity for hard work are also valued.

  • Alignment with University Values: Tampere University prides itself on being a unique, multidisciplinary, and forward-looking community. Candidates are expected to embrace and promote the University's values of openness, diversity, responsibility, courage, critical thinking, erudition, and learner-centredness in their work.

    • Please note that before commencing the position, the selected candidate must apply for a study right within the doctoral program at Tampere University, unless they already have one. Please visit the admissions webpage for more information on eligibility requirements.

Conditions of employment:

  • The position is offered with full funding for a fixed term of four (4) years, commencing in April 2024 or as soon after that as possible. A trial period of six (6) months applies to all new hires.

  • The salary will be based on both the job requirements and the employee's personal performance in accordance with the Finnish University Salary System. A typical starting salary for a Doctoral Researcher is approximately 2700 EUR per month.

Application Process:

To apply, please email your application to Prof. Hamed Badihi (hamed.badihi@tuni.fi). The application package should include:

  • A letter of motivation and description of your research interests (max 1 A4 page) (in English)

  • Curriculum vitae (including the contact details of max two referees and a list of publications, if applicable). References will not be contacted during the initial review process

  • Degree certificates

  • Transcript of records (master's degree studies)

  • Other relevant documents that support your application.

The closing date for applications is 30th of March 2024 (23:59 EET / 21.59 UTC).

Qualified candidates may receive full funding through salaried positions and scholarships. At Tampere University (TAU), it is possible to undertake your entire study program in English.
